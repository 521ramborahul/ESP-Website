Choose Your Adventure

For one Saturday in April, high school students from all over the Chicago area and beyond are welcomed to the Northwestern University campus to take classes from NU students and community members about anything. Thats right, anything. Whether youre interested in science, the humanities, or just random things you may never have heard about, weve got a class for you!

Teacher Vincent Lee advises a student on multispectral image processing during a 3-hour Photoshop course at MIT's Splash 2009. Search/browse a complete list of courses here.

The teachers

Most of your teachers will be undergraduate and graduate students from Stanford University, including:

  • Michael Shaw, Physics graduate student
  • Catherine Lu, Chemistry undergraduate student
  • Pallav Agrawal, Civil/Environmental Engineering graduate student

The subjects

Teachers design their own courses with the aid of student volunteers and training materials provided by Learning Unlimited.

The logistics

The next Splash is on Saturday and Sunday, November 19-20. Anyone enrolled in 7th through 12th grade may attend. Registration is not open yet, but you may create an account to subscribe to e-mail or text message notifications.
